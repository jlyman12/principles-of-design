Focal Point/Emphasis

"Sometimes called the principle of emphasis, the focal point is the center of attention in the design or layout. Another term for focal point is center of visual interest or CVI because it focuses the eyeâ€™s attention" (p.50).

The focal point is where your eyes go when looking at a design online or in print. It is the first thing you see. The designer attempts to draw your attention to a certain point on the page. If you don't have one, viewers won't know where their eyes should go and you won't grab their attention.

iPhone Picture

Eyes need to look somewhere. On your design, that needs to be the most important aspect or image that you want your viewer to see. In life, the focal point can be eyes when talking with someone, the word "Stop" on a stop sign, or the intersection of the beams in the image above. Emphasizing a certain element of your design is crucial to showing your users what you want them to see.
